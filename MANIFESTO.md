# The AMP Manifesto

## Why AI Needs Memory

**We are building artificial intelligence without the most fundamental component of intelligence: memory.**

---

### The Problem

Every day, millions of people have conversations with AI assistants. Every day, those conversations disappear.

```
Monday:
  Human: "Hi, I'm working on a robotics project called OpenClaw..."
  AI:    "Great! Tell me about it..."
  (30 minutes of context setting)
  (Finally productive work)

Tuesday:
  Human: "Let's continue our work on OpenClaw..."
  AI:    "I'm not sure what OpenClaw is. Could you tell me about it?"
  Human: (frustration)
```

This is not a minor inconvenience. **This is a fundamental failure of how we build AI systems.**

A human intern who forgot everything every morning would be fired on day two. Yet we accept this from our most advanced AI systems.

---

### The Deeper Problem

It's not just about remembering conversations. It's about:

**Learning from experience.**
An AI that makes a mistake should remember that mistake.
An AI that finds a solution should remember that solution.
An AI that works with you for months should know you better than an AI meeting you for the first time.

**Growing over time.**
A junior developer becomes a senior developer through years of accumulated experience. Why can't AI agents do the same?

**Working as a team.**
Human teams share knowledge. When one person learns something, the whole team benefits. AI agents today are isolated islands of temporary intelligence.

---

### Our Answer: The Agent Memory Protocol

AMP defines three revolutionary concepts:

#### 1. Agent Identity
Every AI agent deserves a persistent identity. Not just a session ID that expires, but a true identity that persists across conversations, across platforms, across time.

An agent with identity can:
- Build a reputation based on past performance
- Develop specialized expertise over time
- Be accountable for its actions

#### 2. Persistent Memory
Inspired by human cognitive science, AMP implements four types of memory:

- **Episodic Memory**: What happened (events and experiences)
- **Semantic Memory**: What I know (facts and concepts)
- **Procedural Memory**: How to do things (skills and methods)
- **Emotional Memory**: What worked and what didn't (valenced experiences)

These memories follow the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve — frequently accessed and important memories stay strong, while trivial details naturally fade.

#### 3. Agent Mesh
Agents don't work in isolation. AMP defines how agents communicate, collaborate, and learn from each other.

An agent mesh enables:
- Task delegation based on expertise
- Knowledge sharing across agents
- Team coordination for complex tasks
- Collective learning from shared experiences

---

### What We Believe

1. **Memory is not optional.** It is the foundation of intelligence. Without memory, there is no learning. Without learning, there is no growth. Without growth, there is no intelligence.

2. **AI agents should get better over time.** Not through retraining billions of parameters, but through accumulating experience — just like humans do.

3. **The future is collaborative.** The most complex problems require teams. AI agent teams need the same infrastructure that human teams have: shared knowledge, clear communication, and collective memory.

4. **Standards matter.** The web became great because of HTTP. Email became universal because of SMTP. AI agent memory needs a standard protocol.
